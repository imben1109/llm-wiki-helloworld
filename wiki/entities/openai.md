# OpenAI

AI research company and the developer of the GPT series of large language models, including GPT-3, GPT-4, InstructGPT, and the ChatGPT product.

## Overview

OpenAI was founded in 2015 as a non-profit AI safety research organisation, with the stated mission of ensuring that artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity. Co-founders included [[andrej-karpathy]], Sam Altman, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, and Elon Musk (who later departed the board).

OpenAI introduced the GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) model family, which established the pre-train-then-fine-tune paradigm as the dominant approach in natural language processing. The GPT line progressed from GPT-1 (117M parameters, 2018) to GPT-4 (2023), a multimodal model with state-of-the-art reasoning and coding capabilities.

OpenAI also pioneered the use of **reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)** to align language models with human intent, first applied in the InstructGPT paper (2022) and subsequently in ChatGPT, which reached 100 million users faster than any previous consumer product.

## Key Points

- Co-founded in 2015 by [[andrej-karpathy]], Sam Altman, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, and others
- Developer of the GPT series: GPT-1 (2018), GPT-2 (2019), GPT-3 (2020), GPT-4 (2023)
- Introduced RLHF-based alignment with InstructGPT; productised as ChatGPT (2022)
- Transitioned from non-profit to "capped-profit" structure to attract capital for compute-intensive training
- Creator of the DALL-E image generation models and the Whisper speech recognition model
- API platform allows third-party developers to integrate GPT models into their products
