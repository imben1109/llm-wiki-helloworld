# Anthropic

AI safety company and developer of the Claude family of large language models, founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers.

## Overview

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and other former OpenAI researchers who wanted to pursue a more safety-focused approach to AI development. The company's research agenda centres on making AI systems that are safe, beneficial, and understandable.

Anthropic developed **Constitutional AI (CAI)**, a training technique that teaches models to follow a set of written principles (a "constitution") rather than relying solely on human feedback labels. CAI reduces the volume of human labelling required and produces models that can reason about why a given output is appropriate or not.

The Claude model family is Anthropic's primary product. Claude 3 (2024) was released in three tiers — Haiku (fast and compact), Sonnet (balanced), and Opus (most capable) — and features context windows of up to 200,000 tokens. Claude models are widely regarded for strong instruction-following, careful handling of sensitive topics, and long-context reasoning.

## Key Points

- Founded 2021 by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and former [[openai]] researchers
- Developer of the Claude LLM family: Claude 1, Claude 2, Claude 3 (Haiku / Sonnet / Opus)
- Invented Constitutional AI (CAI) as an alignment training technique
- Claude 3 supports context windows up to 200,000 tokens
- Research focus: interpretability, scalable oversight, and AI safety
